5 Ways to Build Your Immunity!

Shreya Dhopte
06 May 2021 • 7 minutes

Have you ever wondered how your friend gets sick fewer times than you? Or are you that friend who stays fit and healthier than others? Don’t forget to thank your immune system! The immune system acts as a guard of our body. It fights with the harmful substances and the germs which enter the body and kills pathogens. Our immune system protects us against diseases, infection, and helps you recover after an injury. But don’t worry if you are the other friend who easily falls sick and catches diseases faster. Here are 5 ways you can increase your immunity and strengthen your immune system.

Before going ahead we should be aware that developing immunity is a slow process and it takes time. No medicine can guarantee building immunity quickly. But by taking proper measures we can develop our immunity naturally.

1.Food and Dietary Supplements:

Having a balanced diet and taking proper nutrition is necessary for having a healthy body and immune system. These nutrients help the immune system in several ways: working as an antioxidant to protect healthy cells, supporting the growth and activity of immune cells, and producing antibodies. Here are the things you can include in your diet.

● Fruits, green and leafy vegetables, rooty and fibre rich food are good for immunity and the digestive system.

● Include ginger, garlic, turmeric in food items. They are excellent immunity booster and reach in vitamin c and mineral and act as an antibiotic agent .

● Take honey with lemon juice, green tea contains antioxidants and polyphenols in tea to help protect your body against free radical damage.

● Try to avoid junk food and reduce your sugar intake; they act as immunity killers.

● Due to our busy and urban lifestyle, we can use dietary supplements like vitamin C vitamin D vitamin A folic acid,zinc and pre and probiotics to fill the gap between our food and a healthy diet.

But before taking dietary supplements, remember that we need these vitamins and minerals in very less amount if they are consumed in excess then they will be excluded by our liver.

2. Exercise:

Exercise is good for body and brain health. It keeps bones healthy and strong. It can boost your mood and make you feel energetic. Exercise causes a change in
antibodies and white blood cells (WBC). WBCs are the body’s immune system cells that fight diseases. These antibodies or WBCs circulate more rapidly during exercise, this helps detect illnesses earlier. So here are the things you can try out.

● Walking or jogging for 30-45 min is also sufficient.

● Try an outdoor sport you like for example, do bicycling, yoga or dance.

● Do yoga and meditation for 10 mins.

● Join your near Zumba, aerobic class or gym but you should not overdo it.

3. Sleep

Getting an adequate amount of sleep is a must for the body because at night while sleeping our body shuts down all voluntary activities and heals. Your body repairs and renews cells during sleep. Poor sleep patterns affect this process and reduce your immunity.
So just remember!

● At least 7-8 hours of sleep are needed.

● Avoid late-night schedules. Sleeping at a fixed time especially between 11 pm-7 am is more beneficial for the body.

● Make sure to get a good quality of sleep. Avoid poor sleeping patterns and disturbed sleep.

4. Including good habits in our lifestyle:

Jim Kwrik said that “First you make your habits and then your habits make you”. Practising good habits, in the long run, can make a great difference. Here are a few listed below.

● Drinking 4-5 litres of water daily to keep your body hydrated.

● Fix your diet and sleep cycle.

● Eat small meals in multiple gapes.

● Eat Breakfast daily. A lack of healthy breakfast makes your blood sugar levels fall and raises your stress levels.

● Quit bad habits like smoking.

5. Mental health:

Mental health is as important for your emotional and physical health. Take a deep breath and say HAKUNA MATATA because taking stress can weaken your immunity, digestive system and increase the chances of illness. The increasing level of stress hormones such as cortisol or adrenaline is not good for our mental health. Here are a few ways to nurture your mental health.

● Make a self-care routine.

● Talk with your loved ones, spend time with them and share your feelings.

● Write a journal like a gratitude diary or art journal.

● Do what makes you happy and love yourself.

● Asking for help when needed, reaching out for therapy.

● Manage your stress levels by managing your work and personal life and take some time for yourself and reflect on yourself.


Thank yourself for taking little efforts for yourself.
