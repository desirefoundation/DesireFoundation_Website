A journey for treat to the soul

Vidisha Bhattacharya
23 Aug 2020 • 2 minutes

The word Desire stirs up a lot of emotions for me.

I was 18, and very lucky to have known about Desire Foundation from the founder itself, which makes it special in a different way.

I did my research about it and made up my mind to be a part of this.

The first event I attended was Holi. We were to visit an orphanage to distribute happiness in form of gifts to the kids.

That day, when I saw the eyes sparkling with the happiness, I felt, I could work very hard and can never be enough to see the smiles.

There began my journey with Desire Foundation.

Surveys, slum visits and every other event was a vital part of my life now, and when I look back, I see how each one had made me a better person altogether. The first slum visit as a lead, I must share, was Telugu Nagar slum under project Adhyayan, and it was too wonderful to be expressed in words.

Social responsibility, communication, leadership, time management and event management, I learned and shared various of these skills here and I look forward to only more of this!

Yes, social work is fun and I am witness to it.

Desire stands apart from the rest because it's a very close-knit family where interns, coordinators and leads work and play like a family.

Yes, I'm a proud part of this family now, and I only Desire to keep working for DESIRE!

Vidisha Bhattacharya
