To Do Or Not To Do!

Upasana Ghosh
26 April 2021 • 5 minutes

Rajeev helps Rahul out.
Rahul and Rajeev's Coversation, Episode 1

Rahul and Rajeev are cousins. They are 13 and 17 respectively. Rahul is a little confused about what he must and mustn’t do during the pandemic. Rajeev helps him figuring out his dilemma. Let’s hear their conversation! 

Rahul: But why is everyone sharing information on social media regarding oxygen supplies? 

Rajeev: Because, Rahul, the country is facing a serious shortage of medical supplies including the provision of oxygen. The ones affected by COVID are suffering due to this shortage and people are trying to help those in need by sharing information they have gathered and verified! It’s kind of a noble cause, actually. We should do it too. 

Rahul: Can’t the affected people take oxygen from hospitals and nursing homes near them?  

Rajeev: Several hospitals and nursing homes are facing a crisis due to the massive inflow of COVID patients in the past month. In this situation, what would your near and dear ones do other than asking for help? 

Rahul: Oh, I get it. I’m relieved that the vaccines have been made. Now we can take it and lead a normal life again! 

Rajeev: The vaccines have been administered to many citizens across the country. But for the vaccines to reach youngsters, there is still time. First, we are securing the ones with lower immunity. 

Rahul: My parents want to get vaccinated. How can they do that?  

Rajeev: They can register here or look for the same in their localities. 

Rahul: I can’t wait to roam around mask-free after I take the vaccine!  

Rajeev: Don’t dare do it! The vaccines, although tested are not 100% effective on every person. Even after administering the vaccine, we must continue to wear a mask and maintain social distancing. You can know more about the vaccine here.

Rahul: But aren’t we safe with our immunity and the strength provided by the vaccine? Why then should we wear a mask? 

Rajeev: We maybe safe from the strain of the virus. But our near ones might not be! It is for them that we must definitely maintain all the protocols and stay safe from the virus. 

Rahul: I was thinking of convincing our parents to go for a vacation! What do you say? 

Rajeev: It is really irresponsible of you to think this way, Rahul! People are being affected everyday and suffering from the talons of the deadly disease. We have to live responsibly. The government has also released some do’s and don’ts regarding several topics.

Rahul: But I see so many people travelling and having fun! 

Rajeev: Every person is different. It maybe useless to make some people understand the devastating consequences of their actions, but you, Rahul, are a smart boy. You should know that this is not a vacation you’re on. You must be active and work positively by staying indoors and putting on a mask whenever you go out. 

Rahul: What should someone do if I they think they have caught it? 

Rajeev: If someone suffers from a common cold, cough, nausea, vomiting, shortness of breath and fatigue, they must isolate themselves and contact your doctor immediately. That’s how they can prevent the spread of the illness! There are apps that allow everyone to take the test sitting at home. They can also get an online consultation from doctors.

Rahul: What else can I do to stay healthy? I really want to develop my immune system and fight the virus! 

Rajeev: That’s easy. You must stay indoors, keep safe distance from others, put on a mask, go out only when necessary, wash your hands after regular intervals, use sanitizers, take your medication, drink lots of water, take necessary vitamins and stay positive.  

Rahul: What about the money we use? Isn’t that capable of transmitting the disease? 

Rajeev: It’s true. What we can do is try to use online modes of payment like Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, etc. whenever we can and definitely sanitize the notes or coins we bring home. Safety comes before anything! 

Rahul: That seems so easy to follow! I will definitely do these and punch the virus away.  

Rajeev: That’s great Rahul. I hope you will live responsibly from now on and provide a service to the society.
