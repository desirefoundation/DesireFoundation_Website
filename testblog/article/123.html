5 Non-Medical COVID Warriors who changed the World!

Bhagyashree U Rao
07 April 2021 • 3 minutes

Before we begin, this is a reminder for you to get up from your seat, stretch out a little left and right and drink some water. And to nudge your friends around too to do so. If you did already, well done, you are now ready to listen!   

It’s World Health Day.
We’ve now swum through one entire year of a pandemic. If there’s one realization that everyone has had, it’s that being in health is not easily attainable for all. Social determinants of health have emerged more evident these days.  

Hence,
the WHO has declared this year’s theme: “Building a fairer, healthier world”, promoting health and well-being for all. 

We now bring to you 5 instances of health action plans by ingenious people during COVID-19: 

1. Disseminating pandemic awareness in rural areas

When the spread was only beginning to emerge in the country, SSP, Amritsar (rural) VikramJeet Duggal spearheaded awar eness drives to alert people against the virus. By monitoring daily stats, having meetings with his force, planning activities and distributing pamphlets, he appealed to the masses to have patience and to cooperate with the police and govt.   

2. Tackling mental health issues

Innerhour, Trijog, ePsyclinic, YourDOST, and Wysa are some startups that helped people with anxiety and depression during the lockdown while making efforts to make mental health issues tangible to Indian societies and tackling the stigma associated with it. 

3. Supporting small food producers and supplying the vulnerable

Melina Taprantzi’s business, Wise Greece, delivers food boxes to the needy from small-scale producers. When the pandemic came, she partnered with MNCs to provide these boxes to the vulnerable and the elderly who couldn’t leave their homes, delivering around 6 tons of food.   

4. Prioritizing rural patients by phone

Around the world, the pandemic magnified rural people’s problems of finding affordable healthcare. Pakistani entrepreneur Maliha Khalid runs Doctory, a hotline service for direct doctor appointments. In 2020, she launched Pakistan’s National COVID-19 Helpline, connecting people to fast and reliable care while saving them time and money.   

5. Distribution of sanitary napkins to women in slums 

Amidst the lockdown, while ration supplies were distributed easily, sanitary napkins were hard to be found. Two Bengaluru teenagers, Niketa Khanna and Tia Povayya, conducted several distribution drives of pads, helping women during such struggling times. 

Remember to take care of yourself and those around you!
