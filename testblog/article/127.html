The Amazing Dr. Ambedkar

Mohit Raj
13 April 2021 • 4 minutes

Whenever someone thinks of a school, one could imagine a lot of friends, quality education, inspiring teachers and loads of fun. But presently, there exists a very large group of people who are not acquainted with this side of life. AND still there are people who defy to accept the fate and rebel against the system. One person of such kind was Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar. 

Key takeaways from this article:

> The staggering tragedies in the early life of Dr. B.R. Ambedkar.
> His struggle against the caste system.
> His value for education.
> His pivotal role in formulating the Constitution of India.
 

History books are filled with the legends of Dr. Ambedkar: how he swam uphill for the rights he deserved including some very basic rights too. Casteism was so highly practiced at the time that he was not allowed to sit at school to study with upper caste students (rather he had to bring a gunny bag to sit on). He was not even allowed to drink water from public containers. One peon had to pour water from a height on his hand and only then could
he drink. There used to be the times when the peon was not around to pour water to him which was when he coined the phrase 'No Peon No Water'.  

He was also the 14th child of a not-so-well-to-do family. You can only imagine how difficult it must have been to be in his shoes. But even then he was a far sighted child. Who could have known at that time that a child struggling would be making all struggles worthwhile and not just for him or his family alone but for a very large group of oppressed people? He is without a doubt no less than a God for many. 

Despite being the 14th child of his family, Ambedkar had a fire within himself. A fire to rebel. And in his fight against the system, one of the brutal weapons was EDUCATION. He believed that education and knowledge has the power to overturn the reign of wrong-doers. Despite all odds, he studied so hard from the very school that oppressed him that his teachers were so proud of him. One of the teachers (Mr. Mahadev Ambedkar) gave his surname to the little Bhimrao whose initial surname was 'Ambavadekar'. He then not only completed his elementary education (which most of his siblings were not able to) but also went for higher education in some renowned colleges of the World. You ask why would one go so far against all the frictions? It was because of his one true belief that EDUCATION CAN BRING A CHANGE. 

It was only because of his sheer inner motivation that he achieved such heights of success in his life. By the time he grew into a full-grown, knowledgeable man, people acknowledged his calibre and thus he was made the chairman of the Drafting Committee. This was the pivotal structure that helped in the formulation of the Indian Constitution. He, along with other hounourable members, drafted the most important document for a country, the CONSTITUTION, in less than 3 years which is an achievement in itself. This couldn't have been done with motivation alone. Proper education and practical knowledge were both required for the same. In the constitution, he has given a very high importance to the implementation of equality in the field of education, since his fight was based on education itself.
