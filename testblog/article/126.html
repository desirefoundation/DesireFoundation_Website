7 Confounding and Unlauded facts of the Jallianwala Bagh Massacre!

Arshleen Kaur
13 April 2021 • 13 minutes

Heroes of Jallianwala Bagh! You do not reside in our history books, just! You belong to our hearts!

The Jalliawala Bagh Massacre is still capable of sending down shivers through our spines. Be it the merciless slaughter of innocent human lives or the acquittal of those guilty, this day is a black day for every Indian. Let’s mark the 102nd anniversary of this event together by commemorating the lives of the martyrs, knowing more about this day.

1) The Pinnacle of Casualties

There was only one narrow main entrance to the park. Furthermore, the park periphery was covered with high walls which made it impossible for the people to climb and escape. This was the main reason for the high number of casualties as the people were unable to evacuate the place in time. For saving their lives, many people even tried jumping into the well to duck guns but eventually lost their lives.  

2) No public gatherings allowed

A Martian Law was imposed on 12th April 1919, because one of the English Missionary was attacked by an angry mob. Around 6,000-10,000 people were gathered in the park. But the public was never warned about any open fire. 

3) A friendly fiesta 

An annual fete of Baisakhi was being celebrated by the local people. The people were unaware that an open shooting was going to take place and were neither warned about it. 

4) Arms and Ammunitions

Apart from rifles, there were two cars filled up with machine guns. Around 1650 rounds were fired. The firing was only stopped when the soldiers ran out of the rounds. 

5) Shaping the nation 

After the incident, many upcoming events related to freedom were sorted. Gandhiji returned his 'Kaisar-i-Hind' medal and Rabindranath Tagore renounced his knighthood. It was one of the reasons of the Non- Coorperation Movement to arise and come into existence.  

6) The revenge

On 13th March 1940, Colonel Reginald Dyer was shot dead by Udham Singh, member of the revolutionist Ghadar party.  

7) A similar incident 

A less known massacre similar to the Jalliawala Bagh Massacre, known as the Qissa Khwani Bazaar Massacre took place in Peshawar. It sparked due to the arrest of a prominent leader, Abdul Ghaffar Khan. On 23rd April, 400 unarmed protestors were shot dead by the troops of British Government. It led to Khudai Khidmatagar Movement.
